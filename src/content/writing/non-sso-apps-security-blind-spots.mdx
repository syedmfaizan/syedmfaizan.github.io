Non-SSO applications are often not risky because of a single catastrophic flaw. They become risky because they live outside normal visibility and governance paths.

## Common failure modes

- No centralized inventory.
- Unclear ownership.
- Manual onboarding.
- Weak offboarding.
- Limited incident response visibility.

## Principle

If an application cannot be centrally authenticated, it should at least be centrally known.
